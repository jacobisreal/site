About Me

My name is Jacob and I reside in beautiful Houston, Texas. I'm 38 years old and work as a technical support representative for a large Internet Service Provider. I have a dog and a roommate, and I love to cook! My favorite foods are mediterranean food and italian (basically pizza and pasta). I am a student at CoderCamps and will graduate in October of this year. I'm excited to have some extra education and a certification under my belt!

My Background

I have studied at several Universities, including Indiana Wesleyan University, American Intercontinental University, and Colorado Technical University. I am about 20 credit hours away from a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Information Systems. I am an ordained minister, although I do not preach at a church. I specialize in counseling and outreach. I am a cancer survivor and 9/11 attack survivor, activist, speaker, writer, and singer.

Why I Write Code

Since I was a child, computer have been an escape from the world around me. Rather than some less productive past-times, I chose at an early age to utilize computers. When I learned that I could basically make a computer do whatever I want if I spoke its language, I decided to learn the BASIC programming language. I was 10 years old. My first program was a phone book application that stored my friends' phone numbers. Keep in mind, this was in the early 90s, and computer storage was limited to tape cassettes. I learned that if I played the tape, it sounded like what most people would call the "dial-up" sound and I instantly fell in love with the song of data (not to mention it annoyed the hell out of my siblings!).
